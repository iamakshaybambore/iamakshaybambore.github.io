About

BRUSSELS, BE

I work on the physical limits of hydrogen infrastructure. My PhD at the Université Libre de Bruxelles focuses on gaseous hydrogen (GH2) pipelines — specifically, how total pressure losses evolve over years and decades of operation as internal pipe surfaces degrade from hydrogen embrittlement and erosion.

The work sits between two disciplines: computational fluid dynamics, where I run 2D and 3D simulations of pipe flow, and applied mathematics, where I build and validate wall erosion models against real experimental data from a dedicated GH2 pressure-loss test bench.

Before this, I trained as an aerospace and mechanical engineer — a Master's at Sapienza University of Rome, a Bachelor's at VTU Belgaum — and spent three years at Tata Consultancy Services testing enterprise software, which is where I learned to be pedantic about validation, defect tracking, and not trusting a result until it's been checked twice.

My research is funded through the Win4Excellence project, backed by the Walloon Region's SPW Economy Employment Research and the Walloon Region Recovery Plan.
